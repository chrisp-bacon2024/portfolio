Sorting Algorithms

  • Loops through the list, compares adjacent elements and swaps them if they are in the wrong order.

    Time Complexity: O(n^2)
    Space Complexity: O(1)
  • Divides the list into sorted and unsorted portion, and determines the right position of unsorted items in the sorted portion.

    Time Complexity: O(n^2)
    Space Complexity: O(1)
  • Iterates through the list, selects the smallest item in the unsorted portion to place in the sorted portion.

    Time Complexity: O(n^2)
    Space Complexity: O(1)
  • Builds a max heap, and repeatedly swaps the root of the heap with array's last element. Then reduces heap size and heapifies again until the array has been fully covered.

    Time Complexity: O(n log n)
    Space Complexity: O(1)
  • Selects a pivot, and sorts lesser items to the left and greater items right. Then recursively does the same to the left sub-array and the right until sub has length of 1.

    Avg Time Complexity: O(n log n)
    Avg Space Complexity: O(log n)
  • Recursively divides array into sub-arrays (until length is 1) to sort while merging together.

    Time Complexity: O(n log n)
    Space Complexity: O(n)
  • Uses a hybrid of insertion sort and merge sort. Divides array into runs, and sorts with insertion sort, then uses merge sort to merge these runs together.

    Avg Time Complexity: O(n log n)
    Space Complexity: O(n)
  • Sorts algorithm elements by looking at the elements' individual digits, and sorting accordingly.

    Avg Time Complexity: O(d * (n+b))
    Space Complexity: O(n + b)
    d = # of digits, n = # of elements, b = num system base
